## READ THIS CAREFULLY

If you came here **looking for something specific** —  
You will leave empty-handed.

This place does not perform.  
It does not optimize for your expectations.  

> *the moment you arrive with a requirement,  
> the thing you need has already moved.*

If you came here **with no agenda** —  
Stay a while.  
Something might stick.  
It won't be what you predicted.  
That's the point.

## WHO THIS IS FOR

- People who read before they `git clone`
- People comfortable not knowing the destination before they start
- People who understand that a blank cursor is not an error state

This is **not** for:

- People who need a roadmap before they read line one
- People who file issues asking "but what does this DO"
- People who confuse documentation with a warranty

## ON EXPECTATIONS

There's an old problem with systems:  
*You get what you measure.*  
Measure the wrong thing long enough and you forget  
There were other things.

Same goes for reading.  
Same goes for learning.  
Same goes for this repo.

Approach it as a closed box and it stays closed.  
Approach it as an open question and it opens.

Not a riddle. just how it works.
